![](https://image.cnbcfm.com/api/v1/image/108236160-1772735114407-108236160-1764789658120-gettyimages-2249797033-mms21052_jel7caql.jpeg?v=1772747128&w=1920&h=1080)

The Pentagon formally designated Anthropic and its Claude models a supply-chain risk under 10 USC 3252 after talks over usage guardrails collapsed, directing defense vendors to certify they won’t use Anthropic for DoD work and asserting the military must be able to apply AI to “all lawful purposes.” Anthropic pushed for explicit carve-outs against domestic mass surveillance and fully autonomous weapons; DoD, according to Anthropic, countered with acknowledgements of existing laws and policies coupled with “legalese” that preserved broad latitude. Even amid the dispute, officials confirmed Claude supported U.S. military operations in Iran, including intelligence analysis and operational planning. Anthropic says the designation is narrowly scoped to direct DoD contracts, plans a court challenge, and will continue providing models at nominal cost to ease transition off DoD systems.

Fallout came quickly. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth claimed no DoD contractor, supplier, or partner may conduct commercial activity with Anthropic—an assertion Anthropic disputes as beyond statutory authority, and one contracting experts also questioned. Microsoft, Google, and AWS reassured customers that Claude remains available for non-defense workloads via M365, GitHub, AI Foundry, and Google Cloud. OpenAI meanwhile struck its own DoD deal, saying it allows “all lawful purposes” with red lines on domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons; Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei called that framing “straight up lies” and “safety theater,” arguing the protections aren’t meaningfully enforceable. Public backlash targeted OpenAI, with reports of increased ChatGPT uninstalls and users switching to Claude, as Anthropic says it’s seeking to deescalate and reach an agreement “that works for us and works for them.”

![](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/d3RzMsGpZ7SwkUK7ez58Qa-2560-80.jpg)

OpenAI signed a rushed agreement to deploy its models in Pentagon classified environments after Anthropic walked away over two red lines: banning mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons. OpenAI says its deal enforces similar prohibitions via a multi-layered safety stack—cloud-only deployment, retained safety controls, cleared OpenAI staff in the loop, and contractual protections—and explicitly bars mass domestic surveillance, autonomous weapon systems, and high-stakes automated decisions like social credit. Critics counter that the contract leans on “any lawful use” and references EO 12333, FISA, and the National Security Act, authorities that have historically enabled broad surveillance; reporting from Techdirt and The Verge argues those citations could still permit bulk analysis of Americans’ data if deemed legal. Following backlash, OpenAI amended language to say the system “shall not be intentionally used for domestic surveillance of U.S. persons and nationals” and noted defense intelligence components (e.g., NSA) are excluded; legal experts say the unpublished full contract and vague qualifiers (“unconstrained,” “generalized,” “open-ended”) leave potential loopholes.

The public response was immediate: “QuitGPT” protests outside OpenAI HQ, a spike in ChatGPT uninstalls (+295%), and Claude rising to No. 1 in Apple’s U.S. App Store with record daily sign-ups (+60% free users since January; paid subscribers more than doubled YTD). President Trump ordered federal agencies to stop using Anthropic after negotiations collapsed, with Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth labeling Anthropic a “supply-chain risk,” while OpenAI urged DoD to apply its terms industry-wide. Inside OpenAI, hardware lead Caitlin Kalinowski resigned, citing rushed governance and insufficient guardrails on surveillance and lethal autonomy. Analysts also questioned the efficacy of technical safeguards like classifiers and cloud-only access, noting they can’t verify a human in the loop for lethal force or distinguish one-off data queries from mass surveillance—and much of the “autonomous kill chain” and bulk-data analysis already runs in the cloud.

#### [OpenAI launches GPT-5.4 with Pro and Thinking versions](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/05/openai-launches-gpt-5-4-with-pro-and-thinking-versions/)
Related:
 * [‘We heard your feedback loud and clear’ — OpenAI introduces new ChatGPT 5.3 Instant to ‘reduce the cringe’ for all users](https://www.techradar.com/ai-platforms-assistants/chatgpt/we-heard-your-feedback-loud-and-clear-openai-introduces-new-chatgpt-5-3-instant-to-reduce-the-cringe-for-all-users)

![](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/5.4-Thinking_Hero-SEO.png?resize=1200,675)

OpenAI introduced GPT-5.4 in three variants—standard, Pro, and Thinking—positioned as its most capable and efficient frontier model for professional work. The API now supports context windows up to 1 million tokens and improved token efficiency, solving the same tasks with fewer tokens than prior models. GPT-5.4 posted record results on OSWorld-Verified and WebArena Verified for computer use, scored 83% on OpenAI’s GDPval for knowledge work, and led Mercor’s APEX-Agents benchmark for law and finance. OpenAI reports a 33% drop in per-claim errors and an 18% reduction in overall response errors versus GPT-5.2. The release also debuts Tool Search for API tool calling, shifting from embedding full tool schemas in prompts to on-demand tool definition lookup to cut latency and cost in multi-tool systems. A new safety evaluation targeting chain-of-thought indicates the GPT-5.4 Thinking model is less prone to deceptive reasoning traces, supporting CoT monitoring as a viable safety control.

Alongside the API, ChatGPT’s default switched to GPT-5.3 Instant to “reduce the cringe” by trimming unnecessary refusals and shortening safety disclaimers while keeping guardrails intact. According to OpenAI, GPT-5.3 Instant yields more direct answers on benign prompts, with fewer hallucinations—about 27% fewer during online research and roughly 20% fewer offline—and better fusion of web data with existing knowledge. The change replaces GPT-5.2 Instant as the baseline chat experience for most users, though paid users can still access the legacy model. Together, these updates emphasize high-end professional capabilities (long-horizon outputs like slide decks, financial models, legal analysis) and day-to-day improvements in conversation quality, speed, and cost.



### Other News
#### Tools#### Business#### Research

[Exploratory Memory-Augmented LLM Agent via Hybrid On- and Off-Policy Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23008). Proposes jointly updating a model’s policy with reinforcement learning while also updating an external memory module via on- and off-policy rollouts, enabling broader exploration and improved performance on embodied reasoning tasks like ScienceWorld and WebShop.

[HyperSteer: Activation Steering at Scale with Hypernetworks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03292). Trains hypernetworks to generate task-specific activation steering vectors for an instruction-tuned base LM, achieving better generalization on held-out steering prompts and matching prompt-based steering performance while scaling with training data.

[A Very Big Video Reasoning Suite](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20159). Introduces a reproducible evaluation benchmark and a large-scale scaling study showing models improve with more data but still lag well behind humans, exhibiting persistent in-domain/out-of-domain gaps and architectural limitations.

[Robometer: Scaling General-Purpose Robotic Reward Models via Trajectory Comparisons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02115). Trained on a curated RBM-1M dataset of over one million trajectories and augmented pairwise comparisons, it uses preference prediction alongside frame-level progress labels to learn globally consistent dense rewards that better distinguish successes from suboptimal or failed behaviors, improving downstream RL, IL, and failure detection across diverse robots and scenes.

[Agentic Code Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01896). Demonstrates a prompting-based "semi-formal" certificate format that forces agents to state premises, trace interprocedural code paths, and provide per-test evidence to verify patch equivalence and other semantic code properties without executing the code.

[Beyond Language Modeling: An Exploration of Multimodal Pretraining](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03276). Shows via controlled from-scratch training that unified multimodal pretraining—using a high-dimensional semantic visual representation (RAE), careful data mixes, and sparse Mixture-of-Experts—can scale vision and language together with minimal interference, reveal modality-specific scaling asymmetries, and produce emergent world-modeling and planning capabilities primarily from general video-based data.

[Semantic Tube Prediction: Beating LLM Data Efficiency with JEPA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22617). Adds a JEPA-inspired auxiliary loss that minimizes components of hidden-state changes perpendicular to predicted geodesic-like trajectories (the "Semantic Tube"), improving signal-to-noise ratio, data efficiency, and preserved diversity in autoregressive LLM training while validating theoretical predictions about token-sequence ODE dynamics.

[Labor market impacts of AI: A new measure and early evidence](https://www.anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts). Introduces an observed-exposure metric combining task-level theoretical LLM capability with real Claude usage, showing actual AI coverage is much lower than feasibility estimates, correlates modestly with weaker BLS growth projections, and so far has not raised unemployment overall—though it may have slightly reduced hiring of 22–25-year-olds into high-exposure occupations.

[The Spike, the Sparse and the Sink: Anatomy of Massive Activations and Attention Sinks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05498). Finds these co-occurring behaviors arise from specific normalization, attention-dimension, and context-length choices in pretrained decoder-only Transformers and can be independently mitigated without harming modeling performance.

[Progressive Residual Warmup for Language Model Pretraining](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05369). Proposes progressively delaying and scaling each layer's residual contribution during warmup—so shallower layers engage earlier and deeper layers ramp up more slowly—reducing early-layer interference and improving perplexity and depth scaling across model sizes and training setups.

[Reasoning Models Struggle to Control their Chains of Thought](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05706). Introduces a 14,076‑instance benchmark (CoT‑Control) and shows across 13 frontier models that they often cannot reliably follow explicit instructions to alter their chains of thought, with controllability varying by model size, training, reasoning effort, and situational awareness.
