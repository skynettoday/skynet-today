#### [MiniMax Releases MiniMax M2: A Mini Open Model Built for Max Coding and Agentic Workflows at 8% Claude Sonnet Price and ~2x Faster](https://www.marktechpost.com/2025/10/28/minimax-open-sources-minimax-m2-a-mini-model-built-for-max-coding-and-agentic-workflows-at-8-claude-sonnet-price-and-2x-faster/)
![](http://www.marktechpost.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/blog-banner-94.png)

MiniMax introduced MiniMax‚ÄëM2, an open MIT‚Äëlicensed Mixture of Experts model tuned for coding and agentic workflows, with weights on Hugging Face in FP32, BF16, and FP8 (F8_E4M3) safetensors. Although the model has 229B total parameters, it routes only ~10B active parameters per token to keep memory and tail latency low during plan‚Äìact‚Äìverify loops across tools like shell, browser, retrieval, and editors. M2 features ‚Äúinterleaved thinking,‚Äù emitting internal reasoning in <think>...</think> blocks that must be preserved across turns; the team warns removing these harms multi‚Äëstep and tool‚Äëuse performance. Deployment guidance covers SGLang and vLLM recipes, Anthropic‚Äëcompatible API endpoints, default inference settings (temp 1.0, top‚Äëp 0.95, top‚Äëk 20‚Äì40), and a launch promo claiming ~8% of Claude Sonnet pricing and roughly 2x speed, plus a limited free trial.

The team prioritized agent‚Äë and code‚Äëcentric benchmarks over static QA. Reported results include Terminal‚ÄëBench 46.3, Multi SWE‚ÄëBench 36.2, BrowseComp 44.0, and SWE‚ÄëBench Verified 69.4 (with scaffold details, OpenHands, 128k context, 100 steps). Compared to MiniMax M1 (456B total, 45.9B active), M2 is a compact sparse MoE (229‚Äì230B total, ~10B active) targeted at agent loops, with explicit tool‚Äëcalling guidance and a chat template. The smaller activation size is cited for better concurrency and steadier latency in long‚Äëhorizon workflows, enabling lower cost and faster iterations while sustaining multi‚Äëfile edits and cross‚Äëtool plans. The repo includes day‚Äë0 serving docs, reproducibility notes, and clear instructions to retain <think> segments in the conversation history.

#### Research

[Scaling Latent Reasoning via Looped Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25741). The authors show that recursively reusing weight‚Äëtied layers with an entropy‚Äëregularized adaptive early‚Äëexit mechanism yields 2‚Äì3√ó parameter‚Äëefficiency gains at scale, matching larger transformers on benchmarks while improving multi‚Äëhop reasoning, safety, and compute‚Äëadjustable performance.

[Beyond Multi-Token Prediction: Pretraining LLMs with Future Summaries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14751). Training an auxiliary head to predict a compact learned summary of a long future window‚Äîrather than multiple independent future tokens‚Äîimproves long‚Äërange reasoning and yields up to ~5% gains on math and coding benchmarks at the 8B scale.

[Supervised Reinforcement Learning: From Expert Trajectories to Step-wise Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25992). This method trains models to produce an internal monologue and take discrete intermediate ‚Äúactions,‚Äù providing a dense, similarity‚Äëbased reward at each step by comparing predicted actions to decomposed expert actions.

[The End of Manual Decoding: Towards Truly End-to-End Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26697). Lightweight prediction heads augment transformers to dynamically set sampling parameters (like temperature and top‚Äëp) at each step so the model controls decoding end‚Äëto‚Äëend, matching or exceeding expert‚Äëtuned baselines and enabling natural‚Äëlanguage steering of sampling.

[Continuous Autoregressive Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.27688). The approach replaces discrete next‚Äëtoken prediction with next‚Äëvector prediction by compressing K tokens into continuous vectors via an autoencoder and using a likelihood‚Äëfree generative head with new evaluation/sampling methods to reduce autoregressive steps and compute.

[Agent Data Protocol: Unifying Datasets for Diverse, Effective Fine-tuning of LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24702). A standardized Pydantic schema and validation tools convert and combine 13 agent datasets into a unified format (ADP), releasing a 1.3M‚Äëtrajectory dataset and tooling that improve agent fine‚Äëtuning across domains and enable cross‚Äëdataset analysis.

[Defeating the Training-Inference Mismatch via FP16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26788). Switching mixed‚Äëprecision from BF16 to FP16 during RL fine‚Äëtuning reduces numerical rounding errors between training and inference engines, removing the need for importance‚Äësampling fixes, improving stability, and narrowing the deployment gap.

[Kimi Linear: An Expressive, Efficient Attention Architecture](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26692). By combining Kimi Delta Attention and Multi‚ÄëHead Latent Attention, this hybrid linear attention mechanism boosts efficiency and often matches or exceeds full attention on several tasks.

[From Memorization to Reasoning in the Spectrum of Loss Curvature](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24256). Analyzing the Hessian via a K‚ÄëFAC approximation, the authors find memorization and forms of reasoning occupy distinct weight‚Äëspace curvature spectra‚Äîmemorized examples align with diverse, often flatter directions when aggregated, while generalization aligns with consistently moderate‚Äëcurvature directions‚Äîand that ablating low‚Äëcurvature (memorization‚Äëassociated) directions can selectively reduce recitation and differentially affect arithmetic/factual recall versus logical reasoning.

[Language Models are Injective and Hence Invertible](https://www.arxiv.org/abs/2510.15511). The authors prove that for standard decoder‚Äëonly Transformers with common random initializations and finite context, different prompts almost surely produce distinct last‚Äëtoken hidden states (so the input can be exactly recovered) and present SipIt, a gradient‚Äëbased algorithm that reconstructs prompts from activations in provable linear time.

[Remote Labor Index: Measuring AI Automation of Remote Work](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26787). This index evaluates AI agents on real‚Äëworld freelance projects‚Äîcomparing AI outputs to human deliverables via manual Elo‚Äëstyle pairwise judgments‚Äîto quantify how much of remote, computer‚Äëbased work current models can automate (currently about 2.5%).
