#### [Anthropic releases Opus 4.6 with new ‘agent teams’](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/05/anthropic-releases-opus-4-6-with-new-agent-teams/)
Related:
 * [Anthropic launches Claude Opus 4.6 as AI moves toward a 'vibe working' era](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/05/anthropic-claude-opus-4-6-vibe-working.html)

![](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/Claude2_Blog_V1-1.webp?resize=1200,675)

Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.6, its most advanced model to date, adding “agent teams” that split complex projects into parallel sub-tasks handled by multiple coordinated agents. The feature is in research preview for API users and subscribers and is positioned to speed execution compared to a single sequential agent. Opus 4.6 also expands to a 1 million token context window, matching Sonnet 4/4.5, enabling larger codebase navigation and long-document processing in a single session. Beyond coding, the release integrates Claude directly into Microsoft PowerPoint via a side panel so users can draft and edit presentations in-app rather than exporting files from the chatbot.

Anthropic frames Opus as evolving from a coding-first system into a broader productivity tool used by software engineers, product managers, financial analysts, and others, aided by Claude Code and Claude Cowork. This is Anthropic’s first major model launch of 2026 after late-2025 releases of Opus 4.5, Sonnet 4.5, and Haiku 4.5, with enterprise customers accounting for roughly 80% of its business. Executives describe a shift from “vibe coding” to “vibe working,” as the model sustains longer tasks and produces higher-quality professional outputs, potentially disrupting software and productivity sectors. Market jitters reflect this: investors have been spooked by rapid advances, with the WisdomTree Cloud Computing Fund down over 20% year-to-date.

#### [With GPT-5.3-Codex, OpenAI pitches Codex for more than just writing code](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/02/with-gpt-5-3-codex-openai-pitches-codex-for-more-than-just-writing-code/)
Related:
 * [OpenAI says new coding model helped build itself](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/innovation/openai-says-new-codex-coding-model-helped-build-rcna257521)
 * [OpenAI's new Spark model codes 15x faster than GPT-5.3-Codex - but there's a catch](https://www.zdnet.com/article/openais-gpt-5-3-codex-spark-15x-faster/)
 * [A new version of OpenAI’s Codex is powered by a new dedicated chip](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/12/a-new-version-of-openais-codex-is-powered-by-a-new-dedicated-chip/)
 * [OpenAI launches new macOS app for agentic coding](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/02/openai-launches-new-macos-app-for-agentic-coding/)

![](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Codex-dark-1152x648-1770052639.jpg)

OpenAI released GPT-5.3-Codex, a frontier coding model available via CLI, IDE extension, web, and a new macOS Codex app, with API access coming later. The model outperforms GPT-5.2-Codex and GPT-5.2 on SWE-Bench Pro and Terminal-Bench 2.0, and runs 25% faster while using fewer compute resources, according to OpenAI. While described as “instrumental in creating itself,” the company clarifies the model did not autonomously build itself; early Codex versions helped debug training, manage deployments, and diagnose tests/evaluations across the software lifecycle. GPT-5.3-Codex is positioned beyond code generation to cover debugging, deploying, monitoring, writing PRDs, tests and metrics, copy edits, and user research, with an emphasis on steering mid-task and frequent status updates. It is also the first OpenAI coding model labeled “high-capability” for cybersecurity per the company’s Preparedness Framework.

A research preview of GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark—a smaller, latency-first variant—generates code up to 15x faster for real-time, “conversational” coding, initially limited to $200/month Pro users with separate rate limits. Spark achieves major latency reductions (≈80% roundtrip overhead, 50% time-to-first-token, 30% per-token overhead) via session/streaming optimizations and persistent WebSocket connections, and is served on Cerebras WSE-3 waferscale chips as the first milestone in OpenAI’s multi-year Cerebras partnership. Although Spark underperforms GPT-5.3-Codex on SWE-Bench Pro and Terminal-Bench 2.0 and does not meet the company’s “high-capability” cybersecurity threshold, it completes tasks in a fraction of the time and defaults to lightweight, targeted edits without auto-running tests. OpenAI’s new macOS Codex app orchestrates multiple agents in parallel, supports scheduled background automations and natural-language workflows, and aims to keep users in a tight interactive loop while delegating longer runs to sub-agents—pointing toward dual modes of longer-horizon reasoning/execution and real-time collaboration.

#### [Google Unveils Gemini 3 Deep Think for Science & Engineering](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/google-unveils-gemini-3-deep-think-for-science-engineering)
Related:
 * [Is This AGI? Google’s Gemini 3 Deep Think Shatters Humanity’s Last Exam And Hits 84.6% On ARC-AGI-2 Performance Today](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/02/12/is-this-agi-googles-gemini-3-deep-think-shatters-humanitys-last-exam-and-hits-84-6-on-arc-agi-2-performance-today/)

![](https://charming-card-d91ad3487b.media.strapiapp.com/file_8c26002ebf.png)

Google unveiled a major upgrade to Gemini 3 Deep Think, a specialized “extended reasoning” mode optimized for science, research, and engineering where accuracy trumps speed. The update emphasizes scaled test-time compute, internal verification, and multi-step chain-of-thought to reduce hallucinations and improve logical consistency. On benchmarks, Google reports 84.6% on ARC-AGI-2 (verified by the ARC Prize Foundation), a sharp jump over prior models and above typical human performance (~60%) on its visual reasoning tasks. The model also scored 48.4% on Humanity’s Last Exam without tools, indicating stronger long-horizon reasoning across dense, low-data academic questions in law, philosophy, mathematics, and other specialized domains.

Deep Think’s coding and scientific results are similarly elevated. It achieved a 3455 Elo on Codeforces—“Legendary Grandmaster” territory—demonstrating advanced algorithm design, data structure mastery, and time-complexity optimization, with Google noting a 35% accuracy gain over prior Gemini versions in software engineering tasks. In STEM evaluations, the model hit gold-medal-level performance on the written sections of the 2025 International Physics, Chemistry, and Math Olympiads and scored 50.5% on the CMT-Benchmark for advanced theoretical physics. Practical engineering capabilities include converting 2D sketches into 3D-printable models via code generation and solving optimization problems like thin-film process design, underscoring the system’s agentic tooling and extended reasoning focus as Google positions Deep Think against OpenAI’s o1 and Anthropic’s Claude for enterprise-grade, multi-step problem solving.

#### [Bytedance shows impressive progress in AI video with Seedance 2.0](https://the-decoder.com/bytedance-shows-impressive-progress-in-ai-video-with-seedance-2-0/)
Related:
 * [ByteDance’s new model sparks stock rally as China’s AI video battle escalates](https://www.scmp.com/tech/article/3342932/bytedances-new-model-sparks-stock-rally-chinas-ai-video-battle-escalates)
 * [ByteDance’s new Seedance 2.0 supposedly ‘surpasses Sora 2′](https://www.siliconrepublic.com/machines/bytedances-ai-video-model-seedance-2-0-impress-audience-china-stocks)
 * [ByteDance’s Seedance 2.0 Might be the Best AI Video Generator Yet](https://www.techeblog.com/bytedance-seedance-2-0-ai-video-generator/)
 * [Hollywood isn’t happy about the new Seedance 2.0 video generator](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/14/hollywood-isnt-happy-about-the-new-seedance-2-0-video-generator/)

![](https://the-decoder.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/seedance_20_demo_teaser.png)

ByteDance pre-released Seedance 2.0 to select users on its Jimeng/Jianying “Jimeng AI” platform, showcasing a multimodal video generator that accepts up to 12 inputs at once: nine images, three short videos, and three audio files, alongside text prompts. It outputs 4–15 second clips, now exportable in 2K, reportedly ~30% faster than Seedance 1.5, and with automatically generated sound effects/music and lip-synced dialog. A standout is precise reference control: users can lift camera motion, movement choreography, and VFX from uploaded reference videos, swap or add characters, maintain multi-scene character/camera consistency, and even extend existing footage; example commands map assets like @image1, @Video1, and specific frames into scene layouts. Early testers highlighted smoother camera movement, improved visual consistency, strong motion accuracy, and lifelike characters, with some claiming it surpasses OpenAI Sora 2 and Google Veo 3.1 in practical tests; ByteDance also touts watermark-free outputs, though realistic human faces are currently blocked for compliance. Real-world robustness, costs, and generation times remain unknown, and demo clips are likely cherry-picked.

Markets reacted immediately in China, with media and AI stocks such as COL Group (+20%), Perfect World (+10%), Shanghai Film (+10%), and Huace Media (~+7%) rallying as competition with Kuaishou’s Kling 3.0 intensifies. Meanwhile, Hollywood groups condemned Seedance over alleged rampant IP and likeness misuse: the MPA demanded ByteDance cease “unauthorized use” of U.S. copyrighted works; SAG-AFTRA and the Human Artistry Campaign called it “blatant infringement.” Disney and Paramount sent cease-and-desist letters citing vivid, near-indistinguishable reproductions of characters like Spider-Man, Darth Vader, and Grogu, while viral user posts showed prompts generating Tom Cruise vs. Brad Pitt scenes. ByteDance says global access will follow via CapCut, but questions around guardrails, watermarking policy versus rivals’ visible or metadata watermarks, and safe deployment for professional workflows and deepfake risks remain unresolved.

#### Research

[POPE: Learning to Reason on Hard Problems via Privileged On-Policy Exploration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18779). The method trains policies using oracle-provided trajectories as privileged on-policy exploration data to guide updates on hard tasks, improving reasoning performance.

[Saddle-to-Saddle Dynamics Explains A Simplicity Bias Across Neural Network Architectures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.20607). The authors show that networks progress through a nested hierarchy of saddle points and invariant manifolds, recruiting additional effective units over time so that each stage corresponds to solutions of increasing complexity measured by the minimal number of units (kernels, kinks, heads, or rank) required to express the map.

[Test-time Recursive Thinking: Self-Improvement without External Feedback](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03094). This approach proposes that models can iteratively refine their own outputs at test time using internal reasoning steps, improving performance without requiring external feedback or additional training data.

[KL-Regularized Reinforcement Learning is Designed to Mode Collapse](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.20817). The paper proves that common KL-regularized RL objectives—under typical hyperparameters and reference policies—have globally optimal solutions that are often unimodal, explaining diversity collapse and suggesting simple fixes to encourage multimodality.

[Rethinking the Trust Region in LLM Reinforcement Learning](https://www.arxiv.org/abs/2602.04879). The paper argues that PPO's ratio-clipping is ill-suited for LLMs because it misestimates policy divergence on long-tailed vocabularies, and proposes DPPO, which directly estimates divergence (via efficient Binary and Top-K approximations) to improve stability and performance in LLM fine-tuning.

[Learning to Reason in 13 Parameters](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04118). The authors show that reinforcement learning can fine-tune reasoning behavior with extremely tiny adapters—down to 13 parameters—achieving high math-task accuracy that supervised finetuning cannot match at comparable sizes.

[Reasoning Cache: Continual Improvement Over Long Horizons via Short-Horizon RL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03773). The method iteratively constructs and refines reasoning chains using short-horizon reinforcement learning so models can progressively improve performance on long-horizon tasks beyond their original training limits.

[How Do Transformers Learn to Associate Tokens: Gradient Leading Terms Bring Mechanistic Interpretability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19208). The authors analyze gradient-leading terms to show how attention heads and token interactions can be decomposed into mechanistic components that explain how transformers learn token associations.

[Towards Autonomous Mathematics Research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10177). The paper describes Aletheia, an AI research agent that iteratively generates, verifies, and revises mathematical arguments using a powerful reasoning model, web tools, and novel inference-time scaling to produce verified solutions and several AI-authored or AI-assisted research papers, including resolutions of some Erdős problems.

[Deriving Neural Scaling Laws from the statistics of natural language](https://www.arxiv.org/abs/2602.07488). The authors predict the exponent of LLM data-scaling laws from two measurable linguistic statistics—the decay of next-token conditional entropy with context length and the decay of token-token correlations with temporal separation—and validate these predictions against GPT-2 and LLaMA-style experiments on TinyStories and WikiText.

[On-Policy Context Distillation for Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12275). This method trains students on their own generated trajectories using reverse KL against a context-conditioned teacher so they internalize transient in-context knowledge into model weights, improving task accuracy and reducing hallucinations compared to prior off-policy methods.

[F-GRPO: Don't Let Your Policy Learn the Obvious and Forget the Rare](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06717). The work introduces F-GRPO, a difficulty-aware scaling of group-relative advantages that reduces policy sharpening at practical group sizes to improve pass@k and OOD accuracy without extra rollouts.

[Retrieval-Aware Distillation for Transformer-SSM Hybrids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11374). The method identifies and preserves only the attention heads that perform retrieval (Gather-and-Aggregate), replaces the rest with recurrent SSM heads, and distills the hybrid to yield comparable retrieval performance with dramatically lower attention and SSM state memory and throughput costs.

[Discovering Differences in Strategic Behavior Between Humans and LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10324). Experiments using automated symbolic model discovery on iterated rock-paper-scissors show that frontier LLMs identify and exploit opponent patterns earlier and maintain larger opponent models than humans, yielding higher win rates, while smaller models perform worse.
